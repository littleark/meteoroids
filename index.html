Bolide

The word bolide comes from Greek βολίς bolis, which means missile. Astronomers tend to use bolide to identify an exceptionally bright fireball, particularly one that explodes.

Data and Source

The data comes from the The Meteoritical Society and contains information on all of the known meteorite landings. It includes 34,513 recordings of found and fell meteorites. A meteorite is classified as fell if it has been observed by people or automated devices during its fall.
BOLIDES shows all the known fell meteorites that have not been classified as doubtful or discredited.
You can read more about the data at Visualizing.org.
